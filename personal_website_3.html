Other (more productive) stuff I do:


Jiu-Jitsu

I started doing Brazillian Jiu-Jitsu when I was in 8th grade. I stopped during 9th grade due to the pandemic, but I was on my way to becoming a blue belt. My brother and I still spar sometimes (he and I went to the gym together), so I haven't entirely stopped. I'm not doing it as much as I was previously, though.
The picture on the left was taken at a seminar hosted by Grand Master Relson Gracie, when I had only been doing it for a few months. As you can tell, I am extremely unphotogenic.


Robotics:

I'm also a member of the FIRST Robotics team RoboLoCo, 5338. We basically build a robot in 6 weeks to play in a game made of a bunch of different obstacles and challenges. While the upperclassmen tend to do the heavy lifting, that doesn't mean that we underclassmen are useless. What I mostly helped out with (aside from menial labor and cleaning up after meetings) was electrical work - cutting wires, crimping connectors, and some soldering once in a while.
The picture to the left was taken at the only competition we were able to go to during the 2019-2020 season, before it was cut short prematurely by the pandemic. As you can tell, it was quite hype.
